# Crop deficiency & pest symptom patterns

Guardian pairs **structured symptom catalog rows** with **live EC/pH/VPD** from crop profile stages. Output is always **hypothesis + measurable checks** — not a diagnosis.

## Fruiting crops (tomato, pepper, cannabis, eggplant)

- **Interveinal yellowing** on mid-canopy often tracks Mg or pH lockout — confirm feed EC and runoff vs stage targets before changing strength.
- **Tip burn** frequently means EC above profile or salt buildup — measure before adding calmag or bloom boosters.
- **Blossom-end rot** on tomato/pepper fruit is usually **calcium transport** (irrigation rhythm), not only low Ca in the reservoir.

## Leafy & herbs (lettuce, basil, kale)

- **Tip burn** at margins is a common EC-high signal — leafy targets stay lower than fruiting hydro.
- **Wilting** with wet media suggests root stress; with dry media, increase irrigation frequency before raising EC.

## Pest symptom checklists (not pest ID)

- **Chewing holes** — inspect undersides at dusk; mechanical vs insect browse.
- **Leaf spots** — rule out nutrient burn with EC first; then airflow and leaf wetness.
- **White powdery patches** — wipe test (residue vs mildew); improve airflow.

## Operator rule

Never change feed strength from leaf appearance alone. Use **lookup_crop_targets** for numbers and **lookup_crop_symptoms** for ranked hypotheses.
